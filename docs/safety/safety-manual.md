## 1. Company Safety Policy Statement

Building NV is committed to providing every worker — employee and subcontractor alike — with a safe and healthy work environment on every job site, every day. Safety is not a secondary consideration; it is the foundation on which all work is planned and executed.

We believe that all workplace injuries and illnesses are preventable. Achieving a safe job site requires the active participation of every person on site — not just supervisors, not just safety officers, but every worker. Safety is everyone's responsibility.

This company will:

- Provide the equipment, training, and resources necessary to work safely
- Enforce safety rules consistently and fairly, regardless of schedule pressure or project status
- Investigate every incident to understand root causes and prevent recurrence
- Never retaliate against any employee who reports a hazard or safety concern
- Hold all persons on our job sites — employees, subcontractors, and visitors — to the same standards of safe conduct

No task is so urgent that it cannot be done safely. If a task cannot be completed safely, it will not be completed until the hazard is addressed.

## 2. Scope and Applicability

This Safety Manual applies to:

- All W-2 employees of Building NV, regardless of trade classification or role
- All 1099 independent contractors and subcontractors working on Building NV job sites
- All visitors, inspectors, and clients present on any job site under Building NV supervision

This manual applies at all job sites, company vehicles, company facilities, and any other location where Building NV employees are performing work.

**Subcontractor applicability:** Subcontractors are responsible for the safety of their own employees. However, all subcontractor employees must comply with the site safety rules established in this manual while working on Building NV job sites. The GC may remove any subcontractor or subcontractor employee who fails to comply with site safety requirements.

This manual supplements but does not replace applicable OSHA standards and Nevada state law. Where OSHA standards or Nevada regulations are more stringent than this manual, the more protective requirement governs.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

Safe job sites require clear accountability. The following defines safety responsibilities by role.

### Owner / Principal

- Establish and communicate the company's safety culture and expectations
- Provide resources — budget, time, training, and equipment — necessary for safe operations
- Ensure all employees have access to required safety training
- Review all incident reports and drive corrective action
- Set the tone: never permit schedule pressure to override safety requirements

### Project Superintendent

- Primary on-site safety enforcement authority
- Conduct and document weekly toolbox talks (minimum)
- Inspect job site daily for hazards; correct identified hazards immediately or stop work until corrected
- Ensure all workers on site have required PPE and are using it correctly
- Ensure fall protection plans are in place before any work at 6 feet or above
- Conduct or coordinate site-specific safety orientation for new workers arriving on site
- Report all incidents to the owner immediately; complete incident report within 24 hours
- Document all safety inspections, toolbox talks, and training sign-in sheets; retain in site binder

### Foreman

- Direct supervisor of field crew; responsible for day-to-day task execution
- Conduct pre-task hazard identification (job hazard analysis) before starting each new task
- Ensure workers under their supervision use required PPE and follow safe work procedures
- Immediately correct or escalate any safety violation observed
- Report any hazard or near miss to the superintendent the same day it is identified

### All Workers

- Follow all safety rules and procedures established in this manual and as directed by superintendent and foreman
- Wear required PPE for all tasks as specified in this manual and in site-specific instructions
- Report all hazards, near misses, and unsafe conditions to the superintendent immediately — do not wait
- Refuse to perform any task that you believe poses an imminent danger to yourself or others; report refusal to superintendent with reason
- Attend all toolbox talks and required safety training
- Do not operate equipment for which you are not trained or authorized
- Keep your work area clean and organized throughout the workday

### Subcontractors

- Must comply with OSHA standards, all applicable Nevada regulations, and the requirements of this manual while on Building NV job sites
- Are responsible for the safety of their own employees, including providing required PPE, training, and supervision
- Must report any incident or near miss that occurs on a Building NV job site to the site superintendent immediately
- Building NV's superintendent may stop subcontractor work and remove any subcontractor employee who presents a safety hazard or repeatedly violates site safety rules

## 4. Hazard Communication (HazCom)

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200 (Hazard Communication Standard / GHS):

Workers have the right to know about the hazardous chemicals they work with. Building NV maintains a Hazard Communication program that meets or exceeds federal requirements.

### Safety Data Sheets (SDS)

- Every hazardous chemical used on site requires a current SDS (Safety Data Sheet) provided by the manufacturer
- An SDS binder containing SDS for all hazardous chemicals currently in use on the site must be maintained on site and accessible to all workers during their shift — at all times, not just during business hours
- Workers must be able to locate and read the SDS binder without asking permission
- SDS binder location on this site: ___________________________ (posted on site binder cover and near the SDS binder)

### Chemical Labeling (GHS Format)

- All chemical containers must be labeled per GHS format: product identifier, pictogram(s), signal word (Danger or Warning), hazard statements, precautionary statements, and supplier information
- Secondary containers (transferred from original packaging) must also be labeled with product identifier and relevant hazard information
- Never use an unlabeled chemical container; never remove or deface a label

### Training

- All workers are trained on how to read an SDS and understand GHS labels before working with hazardous materials
- Training covers: the location of the SDS binder, how to read each section of an SDS, what the GHS pictograms mean, and what to do in case of exposure
- Training is documented on the SDS/HazCom training sign-in sheet maintained in the site binder

## 5. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is required when engineering controls and administrative controls cannot adequately reduce hazard exposure to acceptable levels. PPE is the last line of defense — not the first.

### General Requirements

- The company provides all required PPE to employees at no cost; workers are responsible for using and properly maintaining issued PPE
- PPE must be inspected before each use; damaged, broken, or worn PPE must be removed from service and reported to the superintendent immediately for replacement
- Wearing PPE is not optional — failure to use required PPE is a safety violation subject to disciplinary action

### Required PPE by Task

| Task | Required PPE |
|------|-------------|
| All site work | Hard hat (ANSI Z89.1 Class E minimum), safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1), steel-toed boots (ASTM F2413 or equivalent) |
| Cutting / grinding | Face shield (over safety glasses), hearing protection (plugs or muffs) |
| Working at heights (6+ feet) | Hard hat, full-body harness with appropriate lanyard or SRL when PFAS is the fall protection method |
| Working with chemicals or solvents | Chemical-resistant gloves (appropriate for the specific chemical — see SDS), chemical splash eye protection, respirator if ventilation is inadequate per SDS requirements |
| Concrete and masonry work | N95 or higher respirator, knee pads for kneeling work |
| Electrical work | Insulated gloves rated for the voltage, insulated tools, arc flash PPE as required by the scope of work |
| Demolition | Hard hat, safety glasses, N95 dust mask minimum, hearing protection |
| Roofing | Hard hat, non-slip footwear, harness and lanyard tied to an approved anchor point |

### PPE Issue Log

All PPE issued to an employee must be documented on the PPE Issue Log (Appendix A), which is maintained in the employee's personnel file. Workers sign the log upon receiving PPE, acknowledging receipt and condition.

## 6. Fall Protection

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart M:

Falls are the leading cause of death in construction. Building NV takes fall protection seriously and will not compromise on these requirements.

### Threshold

Fall protection is required for all work at heights of **6 feet or more** above a lower level. There are no exceptions.

### Acceptable Fall Protection Systems

**Guardrail systems:**
- Top rail: 42 inches (±3 inches) above the walking/working surface
- Midrail: approximately 21 inches (midway between top rail and floor)
- Posts: maximum 8-foot spacing
- Must withstand a force of at least 200 pounds applied within 2 inches of the top edge in any outward or downward direction

**Personal Fall Arrest Systems (PFAS):**
- Full-body harness (not a body belt) — body belts are prohibited for fall arrest
- Deceleration device (shock-absorbing lanyard or self-retracting lifeline / SRL)
- Anchor point: rated for a minimum of 5,000 pounds per attached worker, or designed by a qualified person as part of a complete system
- Self-retracting lifelines (SRLs) are preferred over shock-absorbing lanyards due to reduced fall distance
- Inspect harness and lanyard before each use; retire any component that has arrested a fall

**Safety net systems:**
- Installed as close as practicable under the working surface, but no more than 30 feet below
- Must extend 8 feet beyond the edge of the working surface
- Tested per OSHA requirements before use and after installation

### Holes and Floor Openings

- All holes and floor openings must be covered or guarded immediately upon creation
- Covers: must support at least twice the weight of employees, equipment, and materials that may be imposed on them; must be secured against accidental displacement; must be labeled "HOLE" or "COVER" in large, clearly visible letters
- Guardrail alternative: standard guardrail system on all sides of the opening

### Fall Protection Plan

The superintendent must document a fall protection plan for each job with work at 6 feet or above before that work begins. The plan must identify: all elevated work areas, the fall protection method selected for each area, anchor point locations (for PFAS), and inspection responsibilities.

### Important Reminders

- Ladders used for access to elevated work areas do not satisfy fall protection requirements on the elevated surface
- Never work at height without fall protection in place — being "just for a minute" does not exempt the requirement
- Immediate termination on first offense: removing fall protection while actively working at height

## 7. Scaffolding

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart L:

### Erection and Modification

- All scaffolding must be erected, moved, dismantled, or altered only by a **competent person** and workers trained in scaffold erection
- No unauthorized modifications to scaffold components or configuration

### Platform Requirements

- Scaffold platforms must be fully planked or decked with no gaps greater than 1 inch between planks
- Minimum platform width for a work platform: 18 inches
- Platforms must be secured to prevent displacement

### Load Capacity

- Scaffolds must be capable of supporting at least **4 times the maximum intended load** without failure
- Do not overload scaffolds with tools, materials, or workers beyond the rated capacity; the rated capacity must be posted or communicated to scaffold users

### Guardrails

- Guardrails are required on all open sides and ends of scaffold platforms that are **10 feet or higher** above a lower level
- Top rail at 38–45 inches; midrail at approximately half that height; toeboards on all sides where tools or materials are present

### Access

- Workers must use ladders or stair towers to access scaffold platforms; do not use cross-braces as access
- Scaffold must extend 18 inches beyond the end of the climbing rung at the top unless secured

### Inspection

- A competent person must inspect the scaffold before each work shift and after any event that could affect its structural integrity (wind, rain, impact)
- Tag any scaffold that has been found unsafe with a "Do Not Use" tag; do not remove tag until competent person clears it

### Weather

- Do not use scaffolding during high winds (superintendent's judgment, with consideration for local weather conditions), electrical storms, or icy/slippery conditions

## 8. Ladders

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart X:

### Inspection

- Inspect all ladders before each use; look for cracked or broken rungs, side rails, or hardware; missing safety feet; bent or deformed components
- Remove from service any ladder that fails inspection; tag it "Do Not Use" and report to superintendent for disposal or repair
- Never attempt to repair a damaged ladder in the field

### Extension Ladders

- Extend 3 feet above the landing surface at the top; secure at top and bottom when possible, or have a worker foot the base during use
- Set at a **4:1 angle** (1 foot horizontal for every 4 feet of vertical height)
- Do not stand on or above the third rung from the top

### Step Ladders

- Open fully and lock spreader braces before use; never use a stepladder in a folded/leaning position
- Do not stand on the top two rungs of a stepladder

### General Ladder Rules

- Face the ladder when climbing and descending; maintain **three points of contact** at all times (two hands, one foot or two feet, one hand)
- Do not carry tools or materials in your hands while climbing; use a tool belt, bag, or hoist
- One person on a ladder at a time; do not have two people on the same ladder
- Metal ladders are prohibited near electrical hazards; use fiberglass

## 9. Excavation and Trenching

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P:

Excavation and trenching are among the most dangerous construction operations. Cave-ins can occur without warning and are frequently fatal.

### Before Digging

- Contact **811 (Call Before You Dig)** at least 2 business days before any excavation — required by Nevada law
- Verify all underground utilities have been located and marked; use hand tools near utility lines

### Competent Person Requirement

- All excavations and trenches must be inspected by a **competent person** before workers enter and after any event that could increase hazard (rain, freeze-thaw cycle, vibration from nearby equipment, cracks appearing in soil)

### Protective Systems

- Trenches **5 feet or deeper** require a protective system: sloping, shoring, or a trench box (shield)
- Trenches **20 feet or deeper** require a protective system designed by a registered professional engineer
- Type of soil determines allowable slopes; competent person must classify soil before determining slope angle

### Spoils

- Excavated material (spoils) must be kept a **minimum of 2 feet from the trench edge** at all times

### Egress

- A ladder, ramp, or other means of egress must be located within **25 feet** of any worker in a trench **4 feet deep or more**

### Water

- Never enter a trench with accumulated water unless a competent person has determined it is safe, water has been removed, and stability has been confirmed
- Do not enter a trench during heavy rain until conditions are re-inspected

## 10. Electrical Safety

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart K:

### GFCI Protection

- All 120V single-phase 15- and 20-ampere temporary power receptacles used in construction must have **GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter) protection**
- Use GFCI outlets, GFCI cord sets, or an Assured Equipment Grounding Conductor Program — GFCI is the preferred method on Building NV sites

### Temporary Wiring

- All temporary wiring must be protected from damage by vehicles, pedestrians, or equipment
- No damaged cords in service; visually inspect extension cords before each use; damaged cords must be removed and replaced

### Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)

- Before servicing or maintaining any electrical equipment: de-energize the equipment, lock out the energy source with a personal lock, and tag with the worker's name
- Never remove another worker's lock; only the worker who installed a lock may remove it
- If multiple workers are servicing equipment, each must apply their own lock (group lockout)

### Overhead Power Lines

- Maintain a minimum clearance of **10 feet from overhead power lines carrying up to 50kV** — for voltages above 50kV, clearance requirements increase
- Do not assume any overhead line is de-energized; treat all overhead lines as live until confirmed de-energized and grounded by the utility
- Cranes, boom lifts, ladders, and materials must maintain required clearance

### Electrical Panels

- Never block access to an electrical panel; maintain a minimum **3-foot clearance** in front of all panels
- Breaker boxes must remain closed and labeled

## 11. Tool and Equipment Safety

### General

- Inspect all tools before use; remove from service if damaged, modified, or missing guards; tag "Do Not Use" and report to superintendent
- Use tools only for their intended purpose; do not improvise
- Maintain tools in good working condition; store properly after use

### Power Tools

- All power tools must have functioning guards in place; never remove or defeat guards
- Disconnect power tools from energy source before changing attachments, blades, or bits
- Do not use electric tools in wet conditions unless rated for wet use

### Portable Grinders

- Use only the correct type and size of grinding wheel for the grinder and material; check wheel for cracks before mounting
- Guard must be in place and adjusted correctly at all times
- Wear face shield over safety glasses when grinding

### Pneumatic Tools

- Use correct, matching hose fittings; never use a hose with a damaged or leaky fitting
- Never point a pneumatic tool at a person
- Disconnect air supply before changing attachments or clearing jams

### Powder-Actuated Tools

- Operators must be trained and licensed per Nevada requirements; licensing cards must be on person while operating
- Always wear face shield over safety glasses when operating
- Never leave loaded tool unattended

### Compressed Gas Cylinders

- Store upright; chain to a wall, post, or cart at all times when in storage or use
- Replace protective caps when not in use
- Store oxygen and fuel gases (acetylene, propane) **20 feet apart minimum** or separated by a non-combustible wall at least 5 feet high with a 30-minute fire rating
- Never drop cylinders or allow them to be struck by vehicles or equipment

## 12. Heat Illness Prevention

Per NAC Chapter 618 and Nevada OSHA guidance:

All outdoor work in Nevada is subject to significant heat illness risk from approximately **May through September**, with dangerous conditions possible on any day where temperatures exceed 90°F. Heat illness can be fatal. Building NV takes preventive action seriously.

### Water

- At least **1 quart of cool water (50–60°F) per worker per hour** must be available at all times during outdoor work in high heat
- Water is provided at no cost and must be located within reasonable walking distance of all work areas
- Electrolyte supplementation (sports drinks or salt tablets) is encouraged during heavy exertion in extreme heat

### Shade

- Sufficient shade to accommodate **all workers on rest break simultaneously** must be available
- Shade is required to be provided when outdoor temperatures reach **80°F** or above
- Shade must be located as close as practicable to where workers are working

### Rest Breaks

- Workers may take a **10-minute cool-down rest in shade** at any time they feel the need; no worker may be discouraged from taking a heat-related rest break
- At temperatures above 100°F, scheduled rest breaks in shade are required; superintendent will establish a rotation based on conditions and work intensity

### Acclimatization

- Workers new to outdoor work in Nevada heat (including returning workers after extended absence) must be **gradually introduced to heat exposure**
- Day 1: limit to approximately 50% of full heat exposure workload
- Increase exposure over a 7–14 day acclimatization period to reach full workload
- Closely supervise new and returning workers during this period

### Warning Signs and Response

**Heat Exhaustion** (treat immediately — move to cool area, provide water, cool the skin):
- Heavy sweating
- Weakness, dizziness, or fainting
- Cold, pale, clammy skin
- Fast, weak pulse
- Nausea or vomiting
- Headache

**Heat Stroke (EMERGENCY — call 911 immediately):**
- Body temperature of 103°F or above
- Hot, red skin (dry or damp)
- Rapid, strong pulse
- Confusion, slurred speech, or unconsciousness
- Do not leave the worker alone; apply cold water or ice to skin while waiting for EMS

### Never leave a worker alone who shows signs of heat illness.

### Training

- Superintendent must conduct heat illness prevention training at the start of each heat season and for any worker newly assigned to outdoor work
- Training covers: risks of working in high heat, importance of water and shade, acclimatization, how to recognize and respond to heat illness, right to take heat-related breaks

## 13. Silica and Dust Control

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1153 (Respirable Crystalline Silica in Construction):

Respirable crystalline silica is generated by cutting, grinding, drilling, crushing, or otherwise disturbing concrete, masonry, rock, or sand. Chronic silica exposure causes silicosis — a progressive, incurable lung disease. It also causes lung cancer.

### Permissible Exposure Limit

The OSHA **Permissible Exposure Limit (PEL)** for respirable crystalline silica is **50 μg/m³** as an 8-hour time-weighted average (TWA). The Action Level is 25 μg/m³.

### Engineering Controls (Preferred)

- Use **water suppression** (wet methods) when cutting or grinding concrete, masonry, or similar materials — direct water at the point of cut
- Use **local exhaust ventilation (LEV)** on cutting and grinding equipment (vacuum systems attached to tools)
- Wet methods are required for concrete cutting whenever feasible

### Respiratory Protection

- When engineering controls alone cannot reduce exposure to the PEL: use an **N95 respirator at minimum**; use a **P100 respirator** for heavy silica-generating operations (saw cutting concrete, jackhammering)
- Respirator use program: workers must be medically cleared, fit-tested, and trained on correct use before using a respirator

### Work Practices

- Designate areas for high-dust work when possible; restrict access during high-dust operations
- **Prohibit dry sweeping** of silica-containing dust — use wet methods or HEPA vacuum
- Never use compressed air to blow off silica dust from clothing, equipment, or surfaces

### Recordkeeping

- Exposure records maintained per OSHA requirements; available to affected workers upon request

## 14. Housekeeping and Site Organization

A clean job site is a safe job site. Housekeeping is a safety requirement, not a preference.

- Keep all work areas clean and free of debris, scrap, and waste materials at all times — not just at the end of the day
- Materials must be stored off the ground where possible and stacked safely to prevent tipping or sliding
- All walkways, stairways, and means of egress must remain clear at all times — blocking an exit path is an OSHA violation and a life safety hazard
- Electrical cords and air hoses must be routed and secured to prevent trip hazards; do not run cords through doorways, windows, or under rugs/carpet
- Combustible and flammable waste (sawdust, solvent-soaked rags, paper, cardboard) must be removed from the work area and placed in appropriate waste containers at the end of each workday
- Tools must be returned to storage, toolbox, or designated tool area when not actively in use — tools left on the ground or at height are a hazard
- At end of each workday: sweep or vacuum work area, collect and bag debris, secure materials against weather or unauthorized access, and confirm all hazardous materials are properly stored and labeled

## 15. Incident Reporting and Investigation

### What Must Be Reported Immediately

The following must be reported to the superintendent the moment the event occurs, who must then immediately report to the owner:

- Any fatality on a Building NV job site
- Any hospitalization of any worker
- Any amputation, loss of an eye, or inpatient hospitalization of any worker
- Any near miss that could have resulted in a serious injury or fatality

### OSHA Reporting Requirements

Federal OSHA and Nevada OSHA (NRS 618.375) require the following notifications:

| Event | Reporting Deadline | How to Report |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Work-related fatality | Within **8 hours** of knowing about it | Nevada OSHA: (775) 688-3045 or osha.nv.gov |
| Inpatient hospitalization (any cause) | Within **24 hours** | Nevada OSHA: (775) 688-3045 or osha.nv.gov |
| Amputation | Within **24 hours** | Nevada OSHA: (775) 688-3045 or osha.nv.gov |
| Loss of an eye | Within **24 hours** | Nevada OSHA: (775) 688-3045 or osha.nv.gov |

Do not wait to gather all information before reporting. Call Nevada OSHA with what you know and provide follow-up as information becomes available.

### Internal Incident Report

All incidents — injuries (however minor), near misses, and property damage events — must be documented on the Building NV Incident Report Form within 24 hours of the event.

Incident Report Form is located: ___________________________ (also available in the job site binder)

Required information on the report:
- Date, time, and exact location of the incident
- Names of all persons involved
- Description of what happened (factual, not speculative)
- Names and contact information of all witnesses
- Immediate cause of the incident
- Contributing factors (what conditions or behaviors led to the incident)
- Corrective action taken or planned

Superintendent and owner review all incident reports within 48 hours.

### OSHA 300 Log

- All work-related injuries and illnesses meeting OSHA recordkeeping criteria must be entered on the OSHA 300 Log **within 7 calendar days** of the owner receiving notification of the recordable event
- OSHA 300A Summary must be posted from **February 1 through April 30** each year in a location where workers can see it
- The OSHA 300 Log is available to employees, former employees, and their representatives upon request

## 16. Drug and Alcohol Policy

Building NV maintains a drug and alcohol-free workplace. Job sites and company vehicles present serious hazards; impaired judgment or coordination can cause injury or death.

### Prohibition

- Workers are prohibited from being under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance while on a job site, in a company vehicle, or performing company business
- Workers are prohibited from possessing, selling, or distributing alcohol or illegal controlled substances on job sites or in company vehicles

### Prescription Medications

- Prescription medications that may affect alertness, coordination, reaction time, or judgment must be disclosed to the superintendent
- Workers taking such medications may be temporarily reassigned to non-safety-sensitive duties during the period of impairment
- Workers are responsible for understanding the side effects of their medications and disclosing relevant information proactively

### Cannabis

- Nevada is a recreational cannabis state. Cannabis use is nonetheless prohibited during work hours and on job sites per this policy
- Cannabis impairment on a job site is treated the same as alcohol impairment

### Post-Incident Testing

- Any worker involved in an on-site incident resulting in injury or significant property damage may be required to submit to drug and alcohol testing as part of the incident investigation
- Refusal to submit to required post-incident testing will be treated as a positive result for purposes of disciplinary action

### Consequences

- First offense (documented impairment): removal from site, written documentation, referral to EAP if available
- Second offense: termination
- Certain situations may result in immediate termination on first offense (see Section 19)

## 17. Emergency Action Plan

This section contains the company-wide emergency framework. Site-specific emergency information — including nearest emergency room, nearest urgent care, local emergency non-emergency numbers, and the designated assembly point — is documented on the **Project Safety Plan** for each job site and posted in the site binder. Workers must familiarize themselves with the site-specific information at orientation.

### Emergency Contact Numbers

| Contact | Number |
|---------|--------|
| All life-threatening emergencies | **911** |
| Poison Control | **1-800-222-1222** |
| Nevada OSHA (incident reporting) | **(775) 688-3045** |
| Owner / Principal | [Name] — [Phone] |
| Nearest ER (site-specific) | See Project Safety Plan |

### Evacuation Procedure

1. Superintendent calls the evacuation ("All clear the site")
2. Workers stop work immediately; leave tools in place
3. Proceed by the designated route to the assembly point (documented on the Project Safety Plan posted in site binder)
4. Do not stop to collect personal items; do not return for tools or equipment
5. Superintendent accounts for all workers at the assembly point
6. Do not re-enter the site until the superintendent announces all clear
7. Superintendent contacts owner and, if applicable, 911 or other authorities

### Fire Response

- Know the location of fire extinguishers on your site before you start work
- **PASS technique:** Pull the pin, Aim at the base of the fire, Squeeze the handle, Sweep side to side
- Attempt to extinguish only small, clearly contained fires where you have a clear escape route; if there is any doubt, evacuate immediately and call 911
- Never block access to fire extinguishers; maintain 18-inch clearance around all extinguishers

### Medical Emergency

1. **Call 911** immediately; provide the site address, nearest cross street, and description of the emergency
2. Send a worker to the site entrance to flag down EMS and direct them to the patient
3. Do not move an injured worker unless they are in immediate danger from additional hazards
4. Provide first aid if trained; if not trained, keep the worker calm, still, and warm until EMS arrives
5. Keep bystanders back to give EMS access
6. Nearest ER and urgent care information: see Project Safety Plan for this job site

### Notification After an Emergency

Following any emergency, the superintendent must:
- Notify the owner immediately
- Preserve the incident scene until released by responding authorities or Nevada OSHA
- Complete an Incident Report Form within 24 hours
- Notify Nevada OSHA within applicable time limits (see Section 15)

## 18. Employee Rights and Anti-Retaliation

Per NRS 618.445:

Every employee working in Nevada under the protection of Nevada OSHA has the following rights:

- The right to **report safety violations** to their supervisor, to the owner, or directly to Nevada OSHA — without fear of retaliation
- The right to **request an OSHA inspection** if they believe a serious hazard exists; workers may request this be kept confidential
- The right to **participate in OSHA inspections**, including speaking privately with OSHA compliance officers
- The right to **see the OSHA 300 Log**, which records work-related injuries and illnesses
- The right to **refuse work that presents imminent danger** without being subject to disciplinary action, provided the refusal is reasonable and the hazard is serious

### Anti-Retaliation

Retaliation against any employee for exercising these rights is strictly prohibited and is a violation of Nevada law (NRS 618.445). Prohibited retaliatory actions include — but are not limited to — termination, demotion, reduction in hours, assignment to less desirable work, and verbal or written threats.

Any worker who believes they have experienced retaliation may report it to:
- The owner/principal of Building NV
- Nevada OSHA: (775) 688-3045 or osha.nv.gov

Building NV will investigate all reports of retaliation and will take corrective action where retaliation is confirmed.

## 19. Disciplinary Policy for Safety Violations

Safety rules exist to prevent injury and death. Violations will be addressed promptly, consistently, and without favoritism.

### Progressive Discipline

| Violation Level | Consequence |
|----------------|-------------|
| First minor violation | Verbal warning (documented in personnel file) and required retraining before returning to the task |
| Second violation OR first serious violation | Written warning (documented), possible removal from job site pending review |
| Third violation OR willful / repeat serious violation | Termination |

A "serious violation" is one that creates a substantial probability of death or serious physical harm (e.g., working at height without fall protection, operating equipment without authorization, ignoring LOTO procedures).

### Immediate Termination Offenses

The following violations result in **immediate termination on the first offense** — no progressive steps:

- Removing fall protection or otherwise working at height without fall protection while actively at elevated height
- Tampering with, bypassing, or removing another worker's LOTO lock
- Reporting to work visibly impaired by alcohol or controlled substances
- Willfully disabling or removing a safety device or guard
- Theft, fraud, violence, or threats of violence on a job site

### Subcontractors

Subcontractor employees who commit safety violations are subject to immediate removal from the job site. The GC may also suspend or terminate the subcontract agreement for repeated violations or willful disregard of safety requirements.

## 20. Safety Training Requirements

Training is required — not optional — and must be documented. Undocumented training is treated as if it did not occur.

| Training | Who | When | Documentation |
|----------|-----|------|---------------|
| Company Safety Orientation (this manual) | All W-2 employees | Day 1 of employment | Signed acknowledgment in personnel file |
| OSHA 10-Hour Construction | All W-2 field workers | Prior to independent site work; within 30 days of hire if not yet certified at time of hire | Card in personnel file; photo of card uploaded to HR system |
| OSHA 30-Hour Construction | Superintendents and Foremen | Prior to assignment to any supervisory role on an active job site | Card in personnel file; photo uploaded to HR system |
| Heat Illness Prevention | All site workers | Annually, before heat season (by May 1 each year) | Sign-in sheet with date and trainer name; retained in safety training binder |
| Toolbox Talks | All site workers | Weekly minimum; more frequent as hazards require | Sign-in sheet maintained by superintendent per job site |
| HazCom / SDS Training | All workers who work with hazardous materials | Before first exposure; upon introduction of new chemical hazards | Sign-in sheet retained in site binder |
| Incident-specific retraining | Workers involved in or proximately related to an incident | Following any incident; before return to the task that resulted in the incident | Documented in the incident report |

**Important:** The OSHA 10-Hour requirement in this table applies to W-2 employees of Building NV. 1099 independent contractors and subcontractors are responsible for the OSHA training compliance of their own employees.

**OSHA 30-Hour reimbursement:** Building NV will cover the cost of OSHA 30 training for superintendents and foremen who do not have current certification. Submit receipts to the owner for reimbursement; the card must be on file before reimbursement is processed.

## 21. Subcontractor Safety Requirements

Building NV's job sites are subject to OSHA 29 CFR 1926 and Nevada OSHA (NRS Chapter 618). All subcontractors working on our sites are expected to meet these standards. Our reputation and our workers' safety depend on it.

### Pre-Work Requirements

Before any subcontractor commences work on a Building NV job site, the following must be on file with the superintendent:

- Current **certificate of insurance** — general liability and workers' compensation coverage at required limits
- Evidence that all site supervisors hold a current **OSHA 10-Hour** certification (card copy)
- Applicable Nevada **contractor's license** and any required trade licenses

### On-Site Conduct

- Subcontractors must comply with all OSHA standards and with the requirements of this Safety Manual while on Building NV job sites
- Subcontractors are responsible for the safety of their own employees; this includes providing all required PPE, training, and supervision
- The Building NV site superintendent has authority to **stop work and remove** any subcontractor employee who presents a safety hazard, repeatedly violates safety rules, or refuses to comply with a safety directive
- Subcontractors must attend and participate in site-wide toolbox talks conducted by the superintendent

### Incident Reporting

- Subcontractors must immediately report any incident involving their employees to the Building NV site superintendent — the same day the incident occurs
- Subcontractors are responsible for their own OSHA 300 recordkeeping for their employees; Building NV may request copies of incident reports involving work on our sites

### Sub-Tier Subcontractors

Any sub-subcontractor engaged by a subcontractor must meet the same requirements. The prime subcontractor is responsible for ensuring their sub-tier complies.

## 22. Document Control and Manual Updates

### Review Cycle

This manual is reviewed and updated on the following triggers:

- **Annual review** — at minimum, every 12 months from the effective date
- **Regulatory change** — when OSHA standards or NRS/NAC 618 regulations change in a way that affects company policy
- **Post-incident review** — when a significant incident reveals a policy gap or inadequate procedure
- **Operational change** — when the company takes on new types of work or equipment that are not covered by existing policy

### Version Control

- Version number, effective date, and approver are documented on the cover page of this manual
- This manual is maintained as a git-versioned file; all changes are committed with a description of what changed and why
- Printed copies are for reference only — always verify you have the current version (check the repository or request a current copy from the owner) before relying on a printed copy

### Employee Notification

- All employees must be notified of significant changes to this manual
- Significant changes require all employees to re-sign the Acknowledgment
- Minor administrative corrections (fixing a phone number, updating a form location) may be made without requiring re-acknowledgment at the owner's discretion
