## 2. Company Overview

### Who We Are

Building NV is a Nevada-licensed general contractor based in Nevada. We specialize in residential and commercial construction and renovation projects across the region. We work with homeowners, developers, and commercial clients who want a contractor that communicates clearly and delivers what they promised.

### How We Operate

We run lean. Every person on our crew is accountable for their work, their safety, and the quality of what we build. We don't have layers of management to absorb mistakes — which means we communicate directly, we show up when we say we will, and we take responsibility when something doesn't go right.

We expect the same from everyone on our team. If you see a problem, say something. If you make a mistake, own it. We fix things faster when we're honest about what went wrong.

### Our Standard

We build things that last. That means doing it right the first time, flagging problems early, and never cutting corners on safety or quality to hit a deadline. A job site we leave is a reflection of this company — we want it to be a good one.

### What This Means

**W-2 Employees:**

- You are covered by company workers' compensation insurance for job-related injuries
- You are subject to all company policies in this workbook and the Safety Manual
- OSHA 10-Hour training is required within 30 days of your hire date if you do not already have current certification

**1099 Independent Contractors:**
- You are responsible for your own tax reporting and payments (self-employment tax)
- You must maintain your own general liability insurance at required coverage levels; proof must be on file before work begins
- You must comply with all site safety rules while working on Building NV job sites
- You are responsible for the OSHA training compliance of your own workers

### At-Will Employment

Employment at Building NV is at-will. This means either party — you or the company — may end the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or advance notice, subject to applicable law. Nothing in this workbook, the Safety Manual, or any other company document creates a contract of employment or alters the at-will nature of your employment.

### Pay Frequency

Pay frequency: ☐ Weekly  ☐ Bi-weekly  (your supervisor will designate at hire)

### Pay Method — Direct Deposit via Gusto

Building NV processes payroll through Gusto. All employees are paid via direct deposit.

**To receive your first paycheck on time, you must:**
1. Watch for an invitation email from Gusto (sent to the email address you provided at hire)
2. Complete your Gusto account setup and enter your direct deposit bank account information **within 48 hours of receiving the invitation**
3. Confirm that your bank information has been verified in Gusto before your first pay date

If you have not received a Gusto invitation within 24 hours of your start date, notify your superintendent immediately.

### Overtime — Nevada Law (NRS 608.018)

Nevada law requires overtime pay at **1.5 times your regular rate** for:

- Hours worked **over 8 in a single workday**, AND
- Hours worked **over 40 in a single workweek**

**Important:** Nevada's daily overtime is calculated in addition to — not instead of — weekly overtime. If you work 10 hours on a Monday, the last 2 hours are overtime regardless of your weekly total. This is more protective than federal overtime law (which only counts weekly hours over 40).

Examples:
- 10 hours on Monday: 8 regular hours + 2 OT hours
- 50-hour week with no day over 8 hours: 40 regular hours + 10 OT hours
- 10-hour day on Monday + 50-hour total week: overtime calculated for both the daily excess and the weekly excess

### Expense Reimbursement

- Job-related expenses must be pre-approved by your superintendent before you incur them
- Submit original receipts to the superintendent within **5 business days** of the expense
- Unapproved expenses may not be reimbursed

## 5. Work Hours and Scheduling

### Standard Hours

Standard hours: _________________________ (your superintendent will communicate project-specific hours)

Typical start times in construction are early morning — often 6:00 AM or 7:00 AM. Expect to adjust based on the project, season, and site requirements.

### Reporting to Work

- Be on site and **ready to work** at your scheduled start time — not pulling into the parking lot, not putting on boots, not looking for your tools. Ready means tools in hand and PPE on
- If you will be late or absent for any reason, contact your superintendent **before your scheduled start time** — no later than 6:00 AM unless otherwise instructed
- Two consecutive no-call/no-show days may be treated as voluntary resignation at the company's discretion

### Attendance Expectations

Regular, punctual attendance is a basic requirement of this job. Construction crews are interdependent — when one person doesn't show, everyone's workday is affected. If you have a scheduling conflict, communicate early. We can usually work around issues we know about in advance; we cannot work around issues that surprise us at job start.

### Weather and Conditions

Outdoor work in Nevada may be affected by extreme heat (see Safety Manual, Section 12), high winds, or rare cold or rain events. Your superintendent will make the final call on work stoppage or modification due to weather. Do not assume work is cancelled because of weather; check your phone for communications from your superintendent before deciding not to come in.

## 6. Code of Conduct

### On the Job Site

- Conduct yourself professionally at all times — this applies to interactions with coworkers, subcontractors, clients, and anyone else you encounter on a job site or while representing this company
- Profanity directed at coworkers, clients, or subcontractors is not acceptable
- No horseplay — job sites contain real, serious hazards. A moment of poor judgment can seriously injure someone
- Personal cell phone use: limit calls and non-work browsing to breaks. Your superintendent has the authority to set stricter rules for specific sites or clients
- Treat all clients' property with respect and care; leave a client's space better than you found it — cleaner, more organized, no damage beyond the intended scope of work

### Social Media

- Do not post photos, videos, or comments about job sites, clients, or projects on social media without **written permission from your superintendent or the owner**
- Do not identify clients by name, address, or other details on social media
- Do not post anything that would embarrass the company, a client, or a coworker — even if you think it's funny
- Building NV's reputation is built on trust and discretion. Protect it.

### Confidentiality

- Client information (names, addresses, project scope, pricing, communications) is confidential
- Company pricing, cost structures, subcontractor rates, and business processes are confidential
- Do not share confidential information with competitors, former employers, or anyone without a legitimate business need to know
- This obligation continues after your employment with Building NV ends

### Conflicts of Interest

- If you are employed by or considering working for a competitor, or if you are starting or considering starting a business that would compete with Building NV while employed here, you must disclose it to the owner before doing so
- Accepting gifts, kickbacks, or payments from vendors or subcontractors in exchange for preferential treatment is prohibited

### Zero-Tolerance Violations

The following behaviors result in **immediate termination on the first offense**, without progressive discipline:

- Theft of company property, client property, or the property of a coworker
- Fraud (falsifying timesheets, expense reports, certifications, or other records)
- Violence or threats of violence toward any person
- Harassment or discrimination on the basis of any protected characteristic
- Reporting to work under the influence of alcohol or a controlled substance

## 9. Training Requirements and Timeline

Training is required, not optional. Building NV documents all training. If training cannot be documented, it is treated as if it did not occur.

### All W-2 Field Workers

| Training | Deadline | How to Complete | Cost |
|----------|----------|-----------------|------|
| Company Safety Orientation (Safety Manual) | **Day 1** of employment | Completed with superintendent at hire | No cost |
| OSHA 10-Hour Construction | **Within 30 days** of hire if not already certified | OSHA Outreach Training Program; online or in-person; search authorized trainers at osha.gov | Approximately $100–$150; Building NV reimburses with receipt |

### Superintendents and Foremen (in addition to above)

| Training | Deadline | How to Complete | Cost |
|----------|----------|-----------------|------|
| OSHA 30-Hour Construction | **Before** assignment to any supervisory role on an active site | OSHA Outreach Training Program; online or in-person | Approximately $200–$300; Building NV reimburses with receipt |

### All Workers — Recurring Training

| Training | Frequency | Who Conducts It | Documentation |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Heat Illness Prevention | Annually, **before May 1** each year | Superintendent | Sign-in sheet with date; retained in safety binder |
| Toolbox Talks | **Weekly minimum** on active projects | Superintendent | Sign-in sheet per session; retained in site binder |

### Reimbursement

Building NV covers the cost of OSHA 10 and OSHA 30 training for W-2 employees who do not have current certification at the time of hire. To be reimbursed:

1. Obtain pre-approval from the owner before enrolling
2. Submit original receipts to the owner within 5 business days of completing the course
3. Your OSHA card (or wallet card photo) must be on file before reimbursement is processed

**Note:** OSHA 10 is a W-2 employee requirement. 1099 independent contractors are responsible for the OSHA training compliance of their own workers.
